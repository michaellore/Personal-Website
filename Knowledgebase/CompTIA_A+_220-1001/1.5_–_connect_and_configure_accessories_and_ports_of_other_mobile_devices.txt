Connecting devices to computers
Micro and mini USB
USB C plug - double sided, can also act as audio output

Apple devices use Lightning - higher power output, double sided, simpler design

Connect mobile phone to PC and use phone for internet connection - tethering or wireless as a hotspot

Early mobile cables were proprietary - many different types, EU helped standardize

NFC - near field communication - use phone to transfer small data wireless over limited area - payment systems, transport, identity card

Bluetooth - PAN personal area network - headsets, health monitors, car integration, smartwatch, speakers

IR Infrared - usually for controlling entertainment center.

Headsets - hands free audio - wired headsets use a 3.5mm connector, apple can use lightning

Speakers - battery powered, bluetooth

Battery packs, either swappable or usb external

Screen and device protectors, may be waterproof, may conflict with docks or wireless charging

Credit card readers can turn phone into payment terminal

SG/microSD card for expanded storage, not available on iOS
